How to Use EverNet Music

Follow these simple steps to add our premium music to your YouTube Shorts

1

Copy the Audio Code

Find your preferred track in our library and copy the unique code using the "Copy" button.

2

Open YouTube App

Launch the YouTube app and tap the "+" button at the bottom to create a new Short.

3

Select Your Video

Choose the video you want to upload and then tap "Add Sound" to add background music.

4

Paste the Code

Paste the code you copied from EverNet into the YouTube search bar to find the track.

5

Confirm Selection

Tap the check button at the bottom right to confirm your music selection.

6

Adjust Volume

Tap the volume icon at the top right to access the volume controls.

7

Set Minimum Volume

Ensure the volume is set to at least 15% for revenue eligibility. This is crucial for monetization.

8

Upload Your Video

Add details for your video and publish it to start earning with EverNet.

EverNet Music Guidelines

Please read carefully to ensure smooth revenue settlement

Content Where Music Cannot Be Used

(Not Eligible for Revenue Settlement)

Music-based Content

If the original video is focused on music (for example: cover songs, instrumental performances, live stage footage, etc.), EverNet music is not allowed.

In other words, if your video already contains music that is not from EverNet, you must not add EverNet tracks.

YouTube may treat this as duplicate or abusive music content, which can lead to:

  • Revenue not being recorded
  • Videos being restricted or flagged
  • Exclusion from revenue settlement

Other Forms of Abusive Content

  • Videos that add music to meaningless or low-effort footage
  • Repeated uploads of extremely short videos
  • Any content that violates YouTube's community or monetization policies

Such content may not be eligible for monetization under YouTube rules.

Using Approved Music Only

EverNet's music library is updated regularly with premium, licensed tracks.

Please use only the music listed on the official EverNet website.

If a track is not listed, revenue may not be counted and the video may be excluded from settlement.

These guidelines exist to protect creators and ensure stable earnings.

Please follow them carefully when using the EverNet music service.
